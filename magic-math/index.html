Finding Hidden Stories in the Numbers

Magic Math

Magic Math is where structured Magic: The Gathering data becomes a story you can explore. Each project starts with source collection and normalization, moves through controlled comparison and validation, and ends with a clear finding supported by an interactive visualization. The first collection examines Commander combo systems, with additional research areas added only after their data and conclusions have been verified.

Combo Systems · first story

Finish Him

Across 15,107 documented lethal Commander combo variants, most don't end with one dramatic finishing move — they end with an instruction to repeat an established sequence. A validated look at how Commander combos actually end, and what that says about the difference between a clean kill card and a damage engine.

what's inside

10 representative finish lines, searchable and filterable

Plus the repeat-driven vs. non-repeat split, a filterable terminal-condition atlas, and a Method Limits section on what the data doesn't claim — all on one self-contained page.

// methodology

Every Magic Math story follows the same discipline: source facts stay separate from derived research proxies, surprising aggregates get checked against representative and outlier records before publication, medians run alongside averages wherever a distribution is skewed, correlation is never presented as causation, and every derived score is explained rather than treated as a black box.

The current (first) collection, Combo Systems, works from a normalized Commander Spellbook combo-variant research pack. Popularity in that data does not prove power, quality, fun, resilience, or social acceptability — it's one measurable signal among several, and it's labeled as exactly that throughout.

// status: first story live, rest of the collection queued

Finish Him is live — the first Combo Systems story to ship, MTG-reviewed and gated before publication. Combo Fame vs. Gravity, the Commander Combo Constellation, the Commander Combo Staging Map, the Combo Long Tail, the Chromatic Combo Codex, the Command-Zone Crucible, and Cheat Code Geometry are all validated against the source research pack but not yet wired into the site, so nothing below claims to be live before it actually is.
