Turning Side Projects Into Proof Of Engineering Judgment

How to make a side project demonstrate judgment and tradeoffs, not just that you can finish things.

Jun 25, 20263 min readBuilder Laboratory
Builder NotesCareerSide Projects

A side project becomes proof when it shows judgment, not just that you can finish things.

The problem

A finished side project shows persistence. It does not automatically show engineering judgment, which is the thing most teams are actually hiring for.

The context

Judgment is visible in tradeoffs: what you chose not to build, how you handled data and failure, how you kept scope and quality under control. A polished demo hides all of that. A documented decision trail reveals it.

The pattern

Treat the side project like a system you own. Write down the problem, the constraints, the tradeoffs, what broke, and what you would do differently. Apply the same quality habits you would use at work: contracts, checks, and scope boundaries.

Example

From Vox Mana: choosing source-first data design over convenience, visual regression that fails for the right reasons, and Kanban handoffs to keep AI-assisted work from drifting. Each one is a documented decision, not just a feature.

Common mistakes

Showing only the polished demo. No writeup of the tradeoffs. Treating the project as a portfolio screenshot instead of a system. Hiding the parts that broke.

My rule of thumb

The feature list shows you can build. The decision trail shows you can be trusted to decide.
