A Better Handoff Template For Long-Running Projects

A handoff structure that transfers decisions and context, not just files and repositories.

Jun 25, 20263 min readBuilder Laboratory
HandoffsDocumentationProject Systems

A handoff should let the next person recover context, not just inherit files.

The problem

Most handoffs transfer artifacts: docs, repositories, tickets. They do not transfer the reasoning behind them. The next person inherits everything that exists and none of the why.

The context

On long-running projects, the expensive thing to lose is decision history: why a path was chosen, what was tried and abandoned, and what is load-bearing. Files survive. Context evaporates.

The pattern

Capture state, decisions, and risk. A good handoff says where things stand, the key decisions and their reasons, what is fragile, what comes next, and where to look. Write it for someone with zero prior context.

Example

A handoff skeleton: current state; key decisions and why; known risks and fragile areas; in progress and next steps; where things live, including repos, docs, and access; open questions. Update it at milestones, not only at the end.

Common mistakes

Writing the handoff only at the very end. Listing files with no reasoning. Assuming shared context that the next person does not have. Skipping the "what is fragile" section entirely.

My rule of thumb

If the next person has to reverse-engineer your decisions, the handoff moved files, not knowledge.
